## The Six Components at a Glance

Every business weakness falls into one of these six categories. They are interconnected, not sequential.

| Component | Core Question | Primary Tool |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Vision** | Where are we going and how do we get there? | V/TO (8 Questions) |
| **People** | Do we have the right people in the right seats? | People Analyzer + GWC |
| **Data** | Are we running on facts or feelings? | Scorecard (5-15 weekly numbers) |
| **Issues** | Can we identify, discuss, and solve problems permanently? | IDS (Identify, Discuss, Solve) |
| **Process** | Have we documented and do we follow our core processes? | Three-Step Process Documenter |
| **Traction** | Are we executing on the vision with discipline? | Rocks + L10 Meeting Pulse |

## V/TO - Vision/Traction Organizer (The 8 Questions)

The V/TO is a two-page business plan that answers eight questions. Every leadership team member must be 100% aligned on all eight.

1. **Core Values** - 3-7 timeless principles that define your culture. Used to hire, fire, review, reward.
2. **Core Focus** - Your sweet spot: Purpose/Cause/Passion + Your Niche (what you do better than anyone).
3. **10-Year Target** - One BHAG (Big, Hairy, Audacious Goal) that gives long-range direction.
4. **Marketing Strategy** - Target Market ("The List") + Three Uniques + Proven Process + Guarantee.
5. **3-Year Picture** - Vivid description: revenue, profit, measurables, and 10-20 bullet points.
6. **1-Year Plan** - Revenue, profit, and 3-7 specific, measurable goals for the year.
7. **Quarterly Rocks** - 3-7 most important 90-day priorities, each owned by one person.
8. **Issues List** - All obstacles, concerns, and opportunities that could block the vision.

## People Component - Right People, Right Seats

**Right People** = share your core values (evaluated with the People Analyzer: +, +/-, or - on each value).
**Right Seats** = GWC filter (Get it, Want it, Capacity to do it - all three must be YES, binary, no gray area).

The **Accountability Chart** defines the structure:
- Every business has 3 major functions: Sales/Marketing, Operations, Finance/Admin
- The **Integrator** sits above these three, harmonizing them
- The **Visionary** sits above the Integrator (dotted line - not every company needs this role)
- Each function has 5 major roles defined
- One person per seat (one person CAN hold two seats, but never two people in one seat)

**The Bar:** Leadership team agrees on minimum acceptable People Analyzer scores (recommended: 3 pluses, 2 plus/minuses, never a minus for 5 core values).

**Three-Strike Rule:** Below the bar? Strike 1 (discuss + 30 days), Strike 2 (discuss + 30 days), Strike 3 (they go).

## Data Component - Scorecard

5-15 weekly numbers that give you an absolute pulse on the business. Built using the "Desert Island" exercise: if you were stranded and only got one sheet of paper per week, what numbers would you need?

Key principles:
- **Activity-based, not results-based** - Track leading indicators (leads generated, appointments set) not trailing indicators (revenue)
- **One owner per number** - If two people are accountable, nobody is
- **Everyone has a number** - From the leadership team down to the receptionist
- **13 weeks visible** - See trends, not just snapshots
- **Off-track numbers drop to the Issues List** - No discussion during Scorecard review, just flag it

## Issues Component - IDS

Three Issues Lists exist: V/TO (long-term parking lot), Weekly Leadership (strategic), Departmental (tactical).

**The IDS Track:**
1. **Identify** - Dig to the root cause. The stated problem is rarely the real issue.
2. **Discuss** - Everyone shares data and solutions openly. Call "Tangent Alert!" when the conversation drifts.
3. **Solve** - Conclude with an action item on the To-Do list.

**Key Rules:** No consensus management (Integrator breaks ties). Be decisive. Enter the danger (the scariest issue is the most important one). Take a shot (propose a solution). Live with it, end it, or change it - no other options.

## Process Component - Three-Step Process Documenter

1. **Identify** your core processes (HR, marketing, sales, operations, accounting, customer retention)
2. **Document** using the 20/80 rule - capture only the high-level 20% that produces 80% of results. Checklists, not novels.
3. **Package** it as "[Your Company] Way" and get **FBA** (Followed By All) through training and management.

## Traction Component - Rocks + Meeting Pulse

**Rocks:** 3-7 absolute priorities for the next 90 days. Specific, measurable, attainable. Each owned by one person. Human beings naturally lose focus after ~90 days, so the 90-Day World resets priorities quarterly.

**Level 10 Meeting** (weekly, 90 minutes, same day/time every week):

| Segment | Time | What Happens |
|---------|------|-------------|
| Segue | 5 min | Good news - transition to working ON the business |
| Scorecard | 5 min | Review numbers. Off-track drops to IDS. No discussion. |
| Rock Review | 5 min | On track / Off track. Off-track drops to IDS. |
| Customer/Employee Headlines | 5 min | Brief good or bad news |
| To-Do List | 5 min | Done / Not done on 7-day action items |
| **IDS** | **60 min** | Solve the most important issues in priority order |
| Conclude | 5 min | Recap To-Dos, cascading messages, rate 1-10 (target 8+) |

## Workshop: Creating and Unfolding Rocks

### Creating Company Rocks (Quarterly Rock-Setting Session)

When Scott says "help me create rocks" or "let's set rocks for this quarter," walk through this process:

1. **Check prerequisites.** Ask: Do you have a 1-Year Plan with 3-7 goals? If not, Rocks will feel arbitrary. You need to know what you're building toward before you can pick the most important 90-day steps.

2. **Brainstorm everything.** Have Scott list every initiative, project, and priority the leadership team wants to accomplish. Get them ALL out. 15, 20, 50 items is fine at this stage. The goal is exhaustive capture, not filtering.

3. **Filter against the 1-Year Plan.** For each item, ask: "Does completing this directly move us toward one of our annual goals?" Items that do not connect to the 1-Year Plan are candidates for the Issues List (parking lot), not Rocks.

4. **Force-rank to 3-7.** Ask: "If we could only accomplish THREE things this quarter that would have the biggest impact, what would they be?" Start there. Add up to 4 more only if they truly qualify. Stop at 7. Everything else goes on the Issues List (not killed, just deferred).

5. **Assign one owner per Rock.** Ask: "Who is the single person accountable for this?" Not a committee. Not "the team." One name. That person can delegate work but owns the result.

6. **Validate each Rock against SMART criteria:**
   - Is it Specific? (not "improve sales" but "close 12 new roofing contracts")
   - Is it Measurable? (binary: done or not done at end of quarter, no gray area)
   - Is it Attainable? (challenging but realistic in 90 days)
   - Does it have a clear completion date? (end of the quarter)

7. **Produce the Rock Sheet.** Output a table:

| # | Rock | Owner | Due Date | Status |
|---|------|-------|----------|--------|
| 1 | [Specific, measurable description] | [One name] | [Quarter end] | Not started |

### Unfolding Rocks (Company to Departments to Individuals)

When Scott says "unfold rocks" or "cascade rocks," walk through this process:

1. **Start with company Rocks.** These are already set by the leadership team.

2. **Each leadership team member carries their owned company Rock(s)** onto their personal Rock list. Then fills remaining slots (up to 7 total) with department-specific priorities that support the company Rocks or address their function's top needs.

3. **Share company Rocks with the whole organization** in a state-of-the-company meeting. Everyone needs to see the vision.

4. **Each department runs the same Rock-setting process** within their team: brainstorm, filter, force-rank to 3-7, assign individual owners. Department Rocks should clearly support company Rocks.

5. **Every employee gets 1-3 individual Rocks** for the quarter. These are the specific, bite-sized contributions that roll up to department and company Rocks.

6. **Validate the line of sight.** For each individual Rock, you should be able to trace: Individual Rock supports Department Rock supports Company Rock supports 1-Year Plan supports 3-Year Picture supports 10-Year Target. If you cannot draw that line, the Rock is disconnected from the vision.

## Workshop: Building and Managing Scorecards

### Creating a Scorecard from Scratch

When Scott says "help me build a scorecard" or "create a scorecard," walk through this:

1. **Run the Desert Island exercise.** Ask: "Imagine you're stranded on a desert island with no phone, no email, nothing. Once a week, someone slides a single sheet of paper under your door with a handful of numbers on it. What 5-15 numbers must be on that paper to tell you whether your business is healthy?" List every category the team needs.

2. **Filter for activity-based numbers.** For each proposed number, ask: "Is this a leading indicator (something we can influence this week) or a trailing indicator (a result we can only see after the fact)?" Replace trailing indicators with the upstream activities that drive them.

   | Trailing (Bad) | Activity-Based (Good) |
   |---|---|
   | Monthly revenue | Weekly proposals sent, appointments scheduled |
   | Customer churn rate | Post-service survey scores this week |
   | Total overtime paid | Daily jobs completed per technician |
   | Quarterly close rate | Weekly demos/estimates booked |

3. **Assign one owner per number.** The owner is the functional leader accountable for that metric, not the person who types it into the spreadsheet.

4. **Set weekly goals.** Each number needs a target. Tie goals to the 1-Year Plan. If the annual revenue goal is $X, what does that mean per week? Work backward.

5. **Build the template.** Output a Scorecard table:

| Owner | Measurable | Goal | W1 | W2 | W3 | W4 | W5 | W6 | W7 | W8 | W9 | W10 | W11 | W12 | W13 |
|-------|-----------|------|----|----|----|----|----|----|----|----|----|----|-----|-----|-----|
| [Name] | [Activity-based metric] | [Weekly target] | | | | | | | | | | | | | |

6. **Expect evolution.** The first cut will be ~85% right. Allow 3 months for the Scorecard to mature into a tool the team loves. Add, remove, or refine measurables as you learn what actually gives you a pulse.

### Managing the Scorecard Week Over Week

When Scott asks about running the Scorecard or troubleshooting off-track numbers:

- **Review weekly in the L10 Meeting** (5 minutes, no discussion). Each owner reports their number. On track or off track. Off-track numbers drop to the Issues List.
- **Display 13 weeks at a glance.** This is where patterns emerge. A single bad week is noise. Three consecutive bad weeks is a trend that needs IDS.
- **Red-flag misses.** Shade off-track numbers in red so they stand out visually. Creates focus and urgency.
- **When a number is consistently off track:** Drop it to IDS and dig to the root cause. Is it the wrong target? Wrong activity being measured? Wrong person owning it? Or a real operational problem?
- **When to change a measurable:** If it consistently fails to give an accurate pulse, or if it does not help predict future results, replace it. The Scorecard is a living tool.

### Turning the V/TO into Reality

The V/TO creates a line of sight from the 10-year vision down to what someone does this Tuesday:

```
10-Year Target (the destination)
  -> 3-Year Picture (what the company looks like on the way)
    -> 1-Year Plan (3-7 goals for this year to stay on track)
      -> Quarterly Rocks (3-7 priorities for the next 90 days)
        -> Weekly L10 Meeting (Rock Review + IDS + 7-day To-Dos)
          -> Daily execution (each person's number + their Rock)
```

**The key insight:** If you are on track for the week, you are on track for the quarter. If you are on track for the quarter, you are on track for the year. And so on up to the 10-Year Target.

When Scott says "how do we actually achieve this V/TO" or "turn this into action," walk through:

1. **Validate the 1-Year Plan.** Are the 3-7 annual goals specific and measurable? Do they clearly support the 3-Year Picture?
2. **Set Q1 Rocks.** Use the Rock-setting workshop above. Each Rock should directly advance a 1-Year Plan goal.
3. **Build the Scorecard.** The weekly numbers should track the activities that drive Rock completion and annual goal achievement.
4. **Launch the L10 Meeting.** This is the weekly accountability mechanism that connects Rocks to execution.
5. **Check alignment.** Every To-Do that comes out of an L10 should trace back up the chain. If the team is generating To-Dos that do not connect to Rocks, they are working on the wrong things.

## Visionary/Integrator Dynamics

For detailed V/I relationship guidance, read `references/eos-complete-reference.md` (Section: Visionary/Integrator Relationship).

**The 5 Rules:**
1. **Stay on the Same Page** - Monthly Same Page Meetings (2-4 hours, off-site, just V and I)
2. **No End Runs** - Employees cannot bypass the Integrator to the Visionary
3. **Integrator is the Tie Breaker** - On day-to-day operational decisions
4. **You Are an Employee When Working "in" the Business** - Visionary follows company rules in their seat
5. **Maintain Mutual Respect** - Never speak negatively about each other to the organization

**Productive Tension:** V and I are Yin and Yang. The friction between them produces better questions and superior solutions when properly managed. The Visionary creates energy; the Integrator channels it.

**Common Failure Modes:** Not letting go, sitting in multiple seats without compartmentalizing, rushing the 1-year ramp-up, false starts (hiring an Integrator but never giving them autonomy).
